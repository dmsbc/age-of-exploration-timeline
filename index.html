Explorers:
Timeline Explorers Artists/Scientists/Engineers Settlements
Christopher Columbus (Sailed across Atlantic in 1492)
Explorer
Christopher Columbus was an Italian explorer who sailed across the Atlantic in 1492 and found the Americas. He made four trips, thinking he had reached Asia. He died in 1506, not knowing he had discovered a new continent.
Amerigo Vespucci (Led a journey to New World ~1500)
Explorer
Amerigo Vespucci was an Italian explorer who traveled to the Americas after Columbus. He realized that the lands he saw were not Asia, but a new continent. The Americas are named after him because of his writings about these discoveries.
Vasco Núñez de Balboa (Found Pacific Ocean in 1513)
Explorer
Vasco Núñez de Balboa was a Spanish explorer known for being the first European to see the Pacific Ocean in 1513. He crossed Panama to reach the ocean, proving that the Americas were separate from Asia. He was later executed due to political conflict in Panama.
Juan Ponce de León (Claimed Florida for Spain in 1513)
Explorer
Juan Ponce de León was a Spanish explorer and conquistador known for his search for the Fountain of Youth. In 1513, he became the first European to land in Florida, claiming it for Spain. He made several trips to the area, but was later wounded in a battle with indigenous people and died from his injuries in 1521.
Ferdinand Magellan (Sailed across Pacific Ocean in 1519 while trying to go around the world)
Explorer
Ferdinand Magellan was a Portuguese explorer who led the first expedition to sail around the world. In 1519, he crossed the Pacific Ocean, but he was killed in the Philippines before completing the journey. His crew finished the voyage, proving that the Earth is round.
Hernán Cortés (Arrived in Mexico in 1519 and wasn't very nice to the natives)
Explorer
Hernán Cortés was a Spanish conquistador who led the expedition that conquered the Aztec Empire in Mexico. In 1519, he arrived in Mexico, eventually overthrowing the Aztec ruler Montezuma. His actions helped Spain gain control of much of the Americas.

Artists/Scientists/Engineers:

Leonardo da Vinci (Made "Mona Lisa" in 1503)
Artist, Scientist, and Engineer
Leonardo is an Italian famous for his art, science, and engineering. His most famous paintings are Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. He also made reserched anatomy and mechanics.
Nicolaus Copernicus (Developed heliocentric theory between 1508 and 1514)
Scientist
A Polish mathematician and astronomer. Copernicus is best known for developing the heliocentric theory, which said that the Earth and other planets revolve around the Sun. This was an idea that changed our understanding of the solar system.
